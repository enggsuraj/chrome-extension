# Privacy Policy — CalmScreen

**Effective date:** April 28, 2026

CalmScreen is a Chrome extension that adds a warmth + brightness layer to web pages. It is designed to be private.

## TL;DR

- **CalmScreen does not collect any data.**
- **CalmScreen does not contact any server.** It has no backend.
- All settings are stored locally in your browser via `chrome.storage.local`.

## What we don't do

- No analytics. No telemetry. No "anonymous usage data."
- No tracking pixels.
- No ad networks. No third-party SDKs.
- No A/B testing.
- No remote configuration.
- No cloud sync.

## What CalmScreen stores locally

Inside `chrome.storage.local`, on your machine:

- Your slider values (brightness, warmth).
- Your selected preset.
- Your list of disabled sites.

You can wipe everything by removing the extension.

## Permissions, in plain language

| Permission | What it's for |
| :--- | :--- |
| `storage` | Save your settings between sessions, locally. |
| `scripting`, `activeTab`, `<all_urls>` | Apply the warmth + dim layer on the page you're viewing. CalmScreen does not read or transmit page contents — it only adds visual overlays. |

## Your rights

Disable, uninstall, or wipe CalmScreen at any time. Because no data ever leaves your machine, there is no remote account to delete.
